Survival is a system. Food, water, heat, blood, stamina and sound all decide whether you reach the next town.

Survivor rule

Stabilize first, take risks later. Greed kills more runs than patience.

Food

  • Early food comes from houses, fruit, infected drops, fishing and animals. Cooking is safer and more efficient than eating raw meat.
  • Keep emergency food but do not hoard so much that stamina disappears.

Water

  • Water pumps are the safest routine source. Containers found in the world should be treated as unsafe until emptied and refilled.
  • Water purification tablets are valuable for remote movement when pumps are not available.

Temperature

  • Wet and cold clothing increases sickness risk. Prioritize insulation, wring out clothes and use fires when necessary.
  • Rain plus low food and water is a common way to spiral into illness.

Blood and health

  • Bleeding must be stopped with clean rags or bandages. Dirty wound treatment can lead to wound infection.
  • Blood loss affects vision and recovery. Avoid follow-up fights until stable.
