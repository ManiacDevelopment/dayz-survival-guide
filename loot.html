Priority loot

  • Knife or blade, warm clothing, bandages, food, water container, rope, matches or lighter, backpack and basic melee weapon.
  • Repair items matter: sewing kit, duct tape and sharpening stone can extend a run.

Civilian loot

  • Houses, sheds and garages are strong early sources for food, tools, clothing, backpacks and car parts.
  • Small villages are often safer than large coastal cities.

Medical loot

  • Hospitals and clinics are worth checking for tetracycline, charcoal, multivitamins, bandages, disinfectant and IV supplies.
  • A compact medical kit is more valuable than extra low-tier guns.

Military loot

  • Military areas improve armor, weapons, magazines and ammunition but attract geared players. Scout before entering.
  • NWAF and Tisy should be treated as combat zones first and loot zones second.
